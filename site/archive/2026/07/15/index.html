Spectrum of Gastrointestinal, Hepatobiliary, and Pancreatic Involvement in Hantavirus Infection: A Narrative Review.

This study aims to review gastrointestinal, hepatobiliary, and pancreatic involvement in hantavirus infection. A comprehensive literature review from 2000 to May 2026 identified GI symptoms as early and common prodromal manifestations of hantavirus disease. It noted that nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea often precede organ-specific complications, with hepatic involvement typically presenting as transient elevations in aminotransferase levels. While current management is primarily supportive, the association of early GI symptoms with more severe disease requires further investigation. Current evidence is limited, mainly derived from small case reports and retrospective studies.

First detection and genetic characterisation of Porcine Circovirus 2 from wild Rattus species in Malaysia

Abstract Porcine circovirus 2 (PCV2) is a major swine pathogen, and recent findings suggest a broader cross-species transmission potential for circoviruses. In a pathogen surveillance program in Selangor, Malaysia, 28 rats and 18 treeshrews were screened. While all samples tested negative for hantavirus, molecular screening unexpectedly detected PCV2 in one wild Rattus norvegicus . Phylogenetic and sequence analyses confirmed the virus belongs to the predominant PCV2d genotype, designated as strain Siew/Rat/Feces/2026. This study reports the first detection of PCV2 in a Rattus species in Malaysia. The detection implies that wild rats might play a role in the environmental circulation of PCV2d in Malaysia, highlighting the importance of continuous surveillance to monitor its ecological distribution and zoonotic potential.
