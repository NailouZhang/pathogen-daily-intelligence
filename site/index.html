汉坦病毒感染中胃肠道、肝胆道和胰腺受累的谱系：叙述性综述。

Spectrum of Gastrointestinal, Hepatobiliary, and Pancreatic Involvement in Hantavirus Infection: A Narrative Review.

This study aims to review gastrointestinal, hepatobiliary, and pancreatic involvement in hantavirus infection. A comprehensive literature review from 2000 to May 2026 found gastrointestinal symptoms to be among the earliest and most common prodromal manifestations, with nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and diarrhea often preceding organ-specific complications. Hepatic involvement primarily manifests as transient elevations in aminotransferase levels. Although current management strategies are mainly supportive, early gastrointestinal symptoms may be associated with more severe disease. Existing evidence is limited, primarily from small studies, highlighting the need for large prospective studies to further understand the mechanisms and prognostic significance of these manifestations.

2026 年邮轮关联的安第斯 hantavirus 爆发及公共卫生应对。

2026 Cruise Ship-associated Andes Hantavirus Outbreak and Public Health Response.

This review examines the 2026 Andes hantavirus outbreak on the MV Hondius cruise ship, summarizing epidemiological features and transmission in a closed, high‑density setting. Public reports and genomic analysis identified 13 cases (11 confirmed, 2 probable) with three deaths, minimal SNP differences indicating limited spillover followed by human‑to‑human spread and an estimated effective reproduction number of 0.7. Advanced age, shared cabins, and close caregiving were key risk factors. The authors highlight rapid detection, international coordination, and risk communication as essential for outbreak control and recommend travel‑history screening and rapid diagnostics for preparedness.
