First nationwide full-genome characterisation of human-derived Andes virus in Chile: a retrospective genomic epidemiology study.

Background: Andes virus (ANDV) is the only hantavirus known to transmit between humans and causes hantavirus cardiopulmonary syndrome in Chile and Argentina. Methods: Retrospective genomic epidemiology study of 58 human clinical samples collected 2011‑2024 in Chile; samples were sequenced for S, M, and L segments and analysed by phylogenetic inference and variant calling, with clinical and epidemiological data obtained from diagnostic laboratories and surveillance programmes. Results: Two major genomic variants were identified: ANDV Chi‑North (central Chile) and ANDV‑South (southern Chile); distinct geographic distributions were observed, but no consistent clustering by clinical severity or recurrent non‑synonymous substitutions linked to severe disease; substitutions previously associated with person‑to‑person transmission in Argentina were not consistently seen in Chilean sequences. Contribution: This is the first nationwide genomic characterisation of human‑derived ANDV in Chile, showing that regional diversification drives viral diversity and that viral genetics alone are unlikely to explain disease severity or person‑to‑person transmission, highlighting the need for continued integrated genomic surveillance. Limitations: not reported in the supplied evidence.
